The iPhone 5S is an improved version of its predecessor iPhone 5. The phone has a similar design to its predecessor, along with a new home button design that uses a laser-cut sapphire crystal. Surrounded by a metal ring, Touch ID, a fingerprint recognition system attached directly to the home button, can be used to unlock the phone and authenticate transactions on the App Store and iTunes Store. The phone also has an improved camera with a larger aperture and dual LED flash that is optimized for different color temperature conditions. iPhone 5S is equipped with the A7 dual-core processor, the first 64-bit (ARMv8-A) processor used on smartphones, comes with an M7 "motion coprocessor", a specialized processor for handling motion data from the phone's accelerometer and gyroscope without the intervention of the main processor.
